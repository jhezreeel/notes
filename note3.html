My Notes / Reflection

Human-Computer Interaction (HCI)

The creative world of UI/UX design and user experience.

Omg, sa HCI super enjoy talaga ako, kasi dito namin natutunan yung basics ng UI/UX design gamit yung Figma. We got to do wireframes, prototypes, animations, and lahat ng paraan para maging mas user-friendly at visually appealing yung system o app.

Honestly, mas masaya ito kesa sa backend coding kasi mas creative siya, more on design and visuals, which has always been my thing kahit bago pa lang ako sa IT. Parang satisfying lang pag nakikita mo yung layout na ginawa mo, tapos gumagalaw pa siya kasi sa mga animations na nilagay mo. Feeling ko legit “alive” yung project mo, and super fun na makita kung paano nage-experience ng user yung ginawa mo.

Key Learning: User Experience

Sa HCI, natutunan ko rin na user experience talaga ang number one. Hindi lang about kung functional yung app; dapat madaling gamitin, intuitive, at aesthetic pa. Kailangan i-consider mo yung perspective ng user—kung paano sila mag-iinteract, ano yung pinaka-natural na flow, at kung paano magiging smooth yung interaction.

Ang best part is yung mga interactive features sa Figma, like hover effects, micro-interactions, at animated transitions. Parang maliit na detalye lang pero huge effect sa feel ng app. Doon ko na-realize na sa UI/UX, details talaga matter—even yung color choice o button placement puwede mag-change ng whole experience ng user.

Mas na-appreciate ko rin yung balance between creativity and functionality. Kailangan hindi lang maganda sa mata, pero efficient din gamitin. UI/UX designers are basically problem solvers using design—iniisip nila kung paano magiging easier, smoother, at mas enjoyable yung experience ng user.

Overall, HCI was super chill and fun. Parang break from all the hardcore coding sa App Dev pero productive pa rin kasi natututo ka mag-imagine at gumawa ng designs na visually appealing at user-friendly. Ang saya talaga pag makita mong mula sa concept hanggang interactive prototype, lahat ng effort mo may impact sa user. If App Dev is all about challenge + reward sa coding, HCI is reward + fun sa creativity—perfect balance sa IT student life.
