My Notes / Reflection

Object-Oriented Programming (OOP)

The turning point in my programming journey: structure, logic, and design.

Grabe, isa sa mga subjects na sobrang nag-enjoy ako this semester ay yung Object-Oriented Programming (OOP). Dito talaga namin na-experience kung paano mag-code nang mas structured at organized, at kung paano gumagana ang mga core concepts tulad ng classes, objects, inheritance, encapsulation, at polymorphism sa totoong projects. Dito ko narealize na programming is not just about making something work, kundi yung logic at design ng system ang pinaka-importante.

Guidance from Sir Elija Equiñas

At siyempre, si Sir Elija Equiñas ang prof namin — sobrang hands-on at chill, pero sabay may laman talaga yung mga tinuturo niya. Hindi lang basta mag-code, kundi tinuturuan niya kami kung bakit ganito ang design ng system, paano dapat mag-structure ng classes, at paano i-handle yung relationships ng objects para efficient at maintainable ang code. Parang nag-eexplore ka ng world ng coding na may purpose at structure.

The First Complete System

Isa sa pinaka-memorable part ng subject na ito ay nung ginawa namin yung first ever system namin with a working database. Grabe, nakakatuwa kasi from scratch, kami mismo yung nagplano, nag-design ng interface, nag-code ng backend, at nag-connect ng database para gumana nang buo. Nung una, sobrang nakaka-overwhelm — ang dami ng kailangang intindihin, lalo na yung connections, relationships ng classes, at logic ng whole system. Pero nung nakita naming gumana siya, ang sarap sa feeling. Parang proud moment talaga kasi nakita namin yung pinaghirapan naming code na finally nagra-run properly at functional.

Na-enjoy ko rin yung part na exploring different concepts of OOP. Dati, akala ko pare-pareho lang ang coding — basta may output, okay na. Pero dito ko narealize na may mas malalim pala: designing reusable code, organizing logic through classes, at pag-structure ng system in a way na madaling i-maintain. Parang nakikita mo na yung skill na yun ay hindi lang for school — para sa real-world coding projects din. Mas naging open-minded ako sa idea na programming isn’t just about syntax, kundi pati planning, creativity, at efficiency sa problem-solving.

Overall, OOP made me appreciate programming even more. Hindi lang basta about typing code, kundi tungkol sa building something functional, reusable, and smart. At dahil sa subject na ito, mas na-inspire akong mag-improve sa coding, mag-explore ng bagong frameworks, at gumawa pa ng mas malalaking projects in the future. Super solid na experience, lalo na dahil guided kami ni Sir Elija all throughout — challenging pero enjoyable, technical pero fulfilling. Parang, looking back, OOP sa 2nd year ko ang pinaka-turning point ko sa programming — dito ko na-feel yung combination ng logic, creativity, at planning na kailangan sa pagiging effective na programmer.
